Stories and Experiences

So far in my coding journey I have experienced and learned so much. These are some of my stories.

Bug Hunting!

Couldn't get my server to load for a frontend assessment. Tried everything for about 25 minutes until I realized that my actual server was loading on the wrong port. I had two different server ports listed. Changed it to all the same port. Bam, works.

Hitting a Wall with JEST

During a QA lab, my pair programming partner and I were hitting a wall with Jest. It was just not working. I started checking our dependencies and realized that our ChromeDriver was not out of date, but too new! Chrome had updated to a beta version that was not compatible with the chromedriver needed to run Jest. Solution: roll back the chrome update to an earlier version that would work and updated the driver needed in the dependencies. And boom, worked.

Crypt-what?

Another concept I learned and was able to explain to a group was crytography. I learned this concept literally on the fly. Who knew about hashing/salting of passwords? I was able to explain the way that this worked and the concept behind how to figure out your password. They were amazed to learn about "brute force" and how long it would take to figure out their password based on what they currently use. It was amazing to hear how many people may or may not use personal information as a password.

Tell me more

I tend to be able to read out code and spit out a pretty decent explanation to someone about what it means and how to go about understanding what exactly it is doing. I have been asked by several people just to take a look and see what is going on with code before. It's a great feeling just for someone to have enough trust in your ability to help steer them in the right direction.
